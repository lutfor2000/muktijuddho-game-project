Liberation War 1971

Muktijuddho

In 1947, India was freed from the British colony and two independent states Hindu-dominated India and Muslim-dominated East and West Pakistan were formed into one country

60% of the total population of Pakistan lived in East Pakistan, whose mother tongue is Bengali, but 40% of the people of West Pakistan declared Urdu as the state language, as a result, the people of East

Pakistan started a movement for Bengali language and the Pakistan Army of West Pakistan shot and killed approximately 26 people in that movement. Not only that, even after partition, the oppressed people of East Pakistan continued to be exploited by the exploiters of West Pakistan. Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Bangladesh Awami League won a majority in the first general election in 1970, but West Pakistan's Yahya Khan rigged the election results and arrested Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. This angered the citizens of East Pakistan. On March 7, 1971, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, in his historic speech, ordered the common people to participate in the freedom struggle. "This time's struggle is the struggle for liberation. This time's struggle is the struggle for freedom." On the night of March 25, under the orders of General Yahya Khan, the Pakistan army carried out massacres in East Pakistan, the war of liberation began...
